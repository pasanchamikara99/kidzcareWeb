CUTTING-EDGE AI-DRIVEN HIGHER EDUCATIONAL AND CAREER ADVISORY PLATFORM

Research Overview

In Sri Lanka, approximately 170,000 candidates qualify for higher education each year, yet many choose their courses based on biased recommendations from family and friends. This reliance on subjective advice often leads to misaligned educational and career paths, adversely affecting students' academic success and professional fulfillment. To address this issue, there is a pressing need for an innovative solution that empowers students to make informed decisions about their futures. This solution should encompass not only course selection but also comprehensive career advisory services, scholarship opportunities, and financial aid guidance.

To tackle these challenges, we propose developing an AI-driven higher education and career advisory platform tailored for Sri Lankan students. This platform will utilize advanced algorithms to analyze students' interests, strengths, and market trends, providing personalized recommendations for courses, career paths, scholarship opportunities, and financial aid options. Additionally, the platform will offer resources for aptitude test preparation to help students enhance their skills and increase their chances of success in higher education. By fostering informed decision-making and empowering students, this project aims to align educational choices with individual aspirations and potential career success.

Literature Survey


AI-driven platforms are significantly transforming educational guidance by offering personalized course recommendations and career advice, helping students make informed decisions that align with their academic and career goals. Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) have been employed in various regions to analyze student preferences, backgrounds, and interests, thereby recommending suitable academic programs. This data-driven approach has proven effective in reducing dropout rates by better aligning students' choices with their future aspirations, ensuring a more tailored educational experience [1], [2]. However, in Sri Lanka, the adoption of such advanced AI technologies remains limited. Many students continue to rely on subjective recommendations, leading to mismatches between their interests and academic paths, further underscoring the need for localized AI-driven educational platforms.

In addition to course guidance, AI technologies like Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) are playing a crucial role in career advisory systems. By leveraging real-time labor market data, RNNs provide students with insights into relevant career paths that align with their skills and the latest job market demands [3]. Despite their success in other regions, tools like these are yet to be widely implemented in Sri Lanka. Furthermore, the use of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) models to provide real-time information on scholarships and financial aid is gaining momentum globally, but its potential remains underutilized locally [4]. Lastly, Optical Character Recognition (OCR) technology, which has been used to automate aptitude testing and improve student readiness for standardized assessments, is also underexplored in Sri Lanka’s educational systems. By integrating these technologies, Sri Lankan students could significantly benefit from enhanced decision-making capabilities and improved access to higher education opportunities.

Research Gap


Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Integration of Diverse AI Technologies

While existing studies explore the efficacy of individual technologies like GNN, GNN,RNN,RAG,OCR in educational settings, there is a lack of research on the integration of these technologies within a single platform. Understanding how these systems can work synergistically to provide holistic recommendations tailored to Sri Lankan students’ needs remains unexplored.

Limited Personalization in Existing Systems

Educational advisory systems frequently offer generic recommendations that overlook students' diverse backgrounds and evolving goals. Implementing data-driven, personalized solutions can empower students to make informed choices aligned with their unique aspirations and preferences.

Lack of Contextualization for Local Environments

Here is a gap in the research focusing on how educational advisory systems can be tailored to local environments, particularly in countries like Sri Lanka. Exploring how to incorporate regional labor market trends, cultural considerations, and available resources will make the advice more relevant and actionable for students.

Research Problem & Solution

Problem Overview

In Sri Lanka, approximately 170,000 candidates qualify for higher education each year, yet many students face significant challenges in making informed educational choices. The prevalent reliance on subjective recommendations often leads to a misalignment between students' interests and their educational paths, contributing to approximately 30% of students changing their majors or dropping out within the first year. Furthermore, nearly 70% of students are unaware of available scholarships and financial aid options, which can significantly impact their ability to pursue higher education. Additionally, the lack of access to reliable career guidance exacerbates the issue, as many students lack the tools necessary to align their academic pursuits with market demands. These challenges underscore the urgent need for a comprehensive, data-driven solution that empowers students to navigate their educational and career paths effectively

Proposed Solution

To address these challenges, we propose developing an AI-driven higher education and career advisory platform tailored for Sri Lankan students. The platform will utilize Graph Neural Networks (GNN) for course recommendations, analyzing relationships between student preferences and available programs to deliver personalized options. For career advice, we will implement Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN) to evaluate students' skills and interests, providing tailored career pathway suggestions based on real-time labor market trends. To assist with scholarship and financial aid guidance, the platform will utilize a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) approach with LangChain. This enables dynamic retrieval and synthesis of information from a comprehensive database of scholarships, financial aid options, and eligibility criteria. By combining traditional data retrieval and generative AI, the platform will provide contextually relevant, up-to-date responses to students'inquiries.Additionally, the platform will include a mock aptitude test feature that leverages Optical Character Recognition (OCR) technology to assess student readiness and improve test-taking skills. By integrating these advanced technologies, the platform aims to empower students to make informed decisions, aligning their educational choices with personal aspirations and enhancing their chances of success in higher education and future careers.

Research Objectives



Personalized University Course Recommendation

The first objective is to develop a personalized course recommendation system utilizing Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), to match Sri Lankan students with higher education courses based on their educational background, preferences, and career goals. This system aims to assist students in making well-informed decisions about their university education.

Generative AI chatbot for Scholarship and Student Loans Guidance

The second objective is to build a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbot that helps Sri Lankan students easily access relevant and accurate information regarding financial aid, scholarships, and student loan opportunities. This chatbot will bridge the information gap in financial support, promoting greater equity in education.

Career Guidance System

The third objective is to create an AI-based career guidance system designed to help Sri Lankan students explore career paths within the IT sector. This system will employ Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN) to recommend personalized career options based on students' qualifications and industry requirements, aiming to enhance students' career decision-making process.

Generating Mock Aptitude Test Questions for Specific Courses

The fourth objective is to develop a system for generating customized mock aptitude test questions, based on historical entrance exam data, using Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and fine-tuning the ChatGPT model. This system will assist students in preparing for exams by providing practice questions tailored to their specific course interests and cognitive abilities.

Methodology

This research focuses on developing an AI-based educational assistance platform for Sri Lankan students, addressing challenges in course selection, financial aid guidance, career path recommendations, and mock aptitude test generation. The platform’s course recommendation system utilizes Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) like GraphSAGE, combining student preferences and academic data to recommend university courses. A Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbot offers personalized financial aid advice, leveraging a Pinecone vector database for efficient information retrieval. The career guidance system, designed with Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), Collaborative Filtering, and Content-Based Filtering, provides tailored career paths in the IT sector. Additionally, a fine-tuned ChatGPT model generates mock law aptitude test questions using OCR for text extraction. These components work together to support students in making informed academic and career decisions, ensuring relevance, accuracy, and accessibility.
